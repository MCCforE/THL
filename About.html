About UND and Grand Forks

Established in 1883, the University of North Dakota is the state’s flagship university. UND has grown into a prominent research university and was recently ranked among the top 100 public universities and the top 25 most innovative universities in the country by U.S. News & World Report. It is also widely regarded as one of the leading universities in the Northern Plains region. UND offers more than 225 fields of study and enrolls nearly 14,000 students. The School of Medicine has major initiatives in rural and public health, and the Aerospace program is home to one of the first and largest unmanned aerial vehicle programs in the world. For more information, please visit und.edu. The photos below are from und.edu.

UND is located in Grand Forks, North Dakota, a vibrant college town of over 57,000 people on the border of North Dakota and Minnesota. Short commutes, a strong public school system, high-quality medical facilities, low crime, ample parks and theaters, and a cost of living below that of large American cities are among its advantages. Ranked as one of the top five best small college towns and America’s best hockey town, Grand Forks offers an excellent quality of life without metropolitan hassles. (und.edu/about/grand-forks.html)

To learn more about living and working in Greater Grand Forks, visit Grand Forks is Cooler.
